01 / The Manifesto

Not a style.
A way of
seeing.

ALFIE began with a question: what does it mean to dress well, without trying? Not the effortlessness of not caring. The effortlessness of having already decided. Of knowing exactly who you are and finding, with relief, that the clothes agree.

"Where the lines between masculinity and femininity are delicately blurred."

Founded in Paris in 2020 by Alice Fresnel, ALFIE draws from 90s minimalism but refuses to be nostalgic. The silhouettes are clean. The proportions are deliberate. And underneath the restraint, there is always something a little subversive. A shirt cut for a man, worn by a woman, made entirely her own. That is the ALFIE paradox. That is the point.

Fashion, at its best, is not decoration. It is a decision. Every ALFIE piece is made to be that kind of decision. The one you reach for without thinking because it already knows what you need.

02 / The ALFIE Woman

She does not dress
for the room.
She dresses for herself.

The ALFIE woman is not a type. She is a sensibility. She moves between work and evening, morning and midnight, without changing more than her mind. Her clothes carry her through all of it, shifting with the light, the mood, the occasion. Never asking her to be more or less than she already is.

She has a keen sense of proportion. She knows the difference between elegance and effort, and she chooses the former every time. There is always something a little cheeky beneath the surface. ALFIE was made for that particular quality. For the woman who understands that restraint and wit are not opposites.

03 / Craftsmanship

Made in Paris.
From the first sketch
to the final seam.

Every ALFIE collection is designed in Paris using paper pattern techniques, a process that demands patience and precision before a single sample is cut. Each style moves from sketch to toile to finished piece through an intricate process of refinement, ensuring that fit, proportion, and construction are exactly right before anything leaves the atelier.

The fabrics come from family-owned mills across Spain, Italy, and Portugal, chosen for their quality, their technical expertise, and their responsible production standards. ALFIE denim is sourced from a trusted Japanese supplier whose understanding of cloth is, quite simply, unmatched. Every finished piece is individually checked at the Paris studio before it reaches you. Not as a formality. As a commitment.

04 / Our Commitment

Responsible by design.
Not by exception.

Materials

ALFIE prioritises upcycled fabrics and deadstock sourced from established luxury houses. These materials are chosen for their quality, their longevity, and the waste they prevent from existing in the first place.

Production

Limited quantities. Always. Not because scarcity is a strategy, but because overproduction is a choice ALFIE refuses to make. Every partner is selected for their quality and their values, in that order.

The Climate Pledge

In 2025, ALFIE became a signatory of The Climate Pledge, committing to reach net-zero carbon emissions by 2040. Ten years ahead of the Paris Agreement. A commitment made not for the press release, but for what comes after it.

Copywriter's Notes
What changed and why

The Manifesto

The live Brand page opens with: "Subtly but profoundly, ALFIE disrupts by finding the perfect equilibrium between femininity and masculinity." That is a thesis statement, not a brand voice. It tells the reader what to think before giving them anything to feel. The rewrite leads with a question and answers it in the same breath: "What does it mean to dress well, without trying?" Then it earns the answer through specificity. The paradox of a shirt cut for a man, worn by a woman, made entirely her own. That is ALFIE. The original never quite said it.

The ALFIE Woman

The live copy describes the ALFIE woman in a way that reads like a brand persona brief rather than a portrait. "A confident, sophisticated woman who values both style and comfort." That sentence describes the customer of approximately every fashion brand that has ever existed. The rewrite earns the specificity that the current version claims without demonstrating. "She does not dress for the room. She dresses for herself." is a line that will make the actual ALFIE woman recognise herself. That is the only job a customer portrait has.

The Craft Section

The live copy reads like a quality control process document. "Each production piece is individually checked at our Paris design studio before shipment to the customer, a final step reflecting ALFIE's considered approach to craftsmanship and design integrity." That is true and important. But it reads like compliance copy, not brand copy. The rewrite gives the same information a different weight: "Not as a formality. As a commitment." Same fact. Completely different feeling. Alice's craftsmanship deserves language that matches its standard.

The Commitment Section

ALFIE signed The Climate Pledge in 2025, committing to net-zero by 2040. Ten years ahead of the Paris Agreement. That is a remarkable fact and it is buried in a paragraph with no emphasis, no weight, no sense of what it actually means. The rewrite gives it a line that earns its significance: "A commitment made not for the press release, but for what comes after it." That is the voice of a brand that means it. The live copy does not yet sound like that brand.

Before and After: The Manifesto Opening
Live copy

"Subtly but profoundly, ALFIE disrupts by finding the perfect equilibrium between femininity and masculinity, presenting the art of menswear for women."

Tells the reader what to think before giving them anything to feel. "Subtly but profoundly" and "perfect equilibrium" are abstract claims that ask to be believed rather than earned.
Rewrite

"ALFIE began with a question: what does it mean to dress well, without trying? Not the effortlessness of not caring. The effortlessness of having already decided. Of knowing exactly who you are and finding, with relief, that the clothes agree."

Leads with a question the reader already has. Answers it in a way that makes her feel understood before she has read a single product description. The brand earns belief rather than demanding it.
